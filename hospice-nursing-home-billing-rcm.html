Hospice billing

Hospice regulatory and billing foundation

Core federal framework

Hospice billing is governed by 42 CFR Part 418, Medicare Benefit Policy Manual Chapter 9, and Medicare Claims Processing Manual Chapter 11.

Payment model

Hospice is paid mainly through per-diem levels of care: routine home care, continuous home care, inpatient respite, and general inpatient care.

Critical hospice timing controls
  • Valid hospice election before billing.
  • Certification of terminal illness, prognosis of 6 months or less, and patient-specific narrative.
  • Oral/written certification within 2 calendar days after hospice care starts for the first period.
  • Notice of Election accepted within 5 calendar days of hospice admission.
  • Initial RN assessment within 48 hours after election completion.
  • Comprehensive assessment within 5 calendar days.
  • IDG plan-of-care review at least every 15 days.
  • Face-to-face encounter generally required before third and later benefit-period recertifications.
Hospice claim-control points
  • Election statement and addendum rights.
  • NOE, NOTR, transfer/change transactions.
  • Level-of-care accuracy: RHC, CHC, respite, GIP.
  • Site-of-service Q500x coding.
  • Related versus unrelated service logic.
  • GV/GW/condition code 07 governance.
  • Late NOE or late certification provider-liability days.
  • Diagnosis integrity: avoid vague principal diagnosis when a definitive terminal diagnosis is known.
SNF / nursing facility

SNF and nursing home billing foundation

Nursing home billing must separate Medicare SNF skilled care from custodial nursing facility care. Medicare generally does not cover long-term custodial nursing home room and board, while Medicaid may cover it under state-specific rules.

Core SNF Medicare Part A controls
  • Medicare coverage basis and qualifying skilled need.
  • Prior hospitalization / 3-day rule where applicable.
  • Physician certification and recertification.
  • MDS/RAI accuracy and iQIES transmission.
  • PDPM/HIPPS payment classification.
  • Consolidated billing review for outside services.
  • Correct professional claim POS 31 versus POS 32.
PDPM/MDS payment logic

PDPM classifies SNF Part A claims using MDS-derived components across PT, OT, SLP, NTA, and nursing categories. Diagnosis mapping, MDS coding, and documentation must reconcile before claim release.

Appeals framework

Medicare fee-for-service appeals generally follow five levels: MAC redetermination, QIC reconsideration, ALJ hearing, Medicare Appeals Council, and Federal district court. Standard clocks include 120 days for redetermination, 180 days for reconsideration, and generally 60 days for later levels. Missing documentation denials may be salvageable; classic untimely filing is usually a dead-end loss.

Compliance

Highest-risk audit and compliance areas

Hospice risks

  • Defective election statements.
  • Incomplete or boilerplate certifications.
  • Weak support for terminal prognosis.
  • Missed face-to-face requirement.
  • Unsupported CHC, GIP, or respite levels.
  • Missing GV/GW/condition code 07.
  • Principal diagnosis integrity issues.

SNF / nursing facility risks

  • Unsupported skilled level of care.
  • Certification failures.
  • Duplicate billing.
  • Consolidated billing mistakes.
  • MDS/PDPM mismatch.
  • Insufficient documentation.
  • False or inaccurate cost/billing reports.

Anti-kickback and vendor-arrangement risk should be reviewed by U.S. healthcare counsel, especially where compensation, referrals, hospice arrangements, or percentage-based fee structures are involved.
